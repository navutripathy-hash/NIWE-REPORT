# Will India Achieve Its Wind Energy Goals by 2030?

### A Data-Driven Research Report with ML Forecasting Framework

This project evaluates India's progress toward its official 2030 wind energy and renewable energy targets using historical data, policy analysis, infrastructure assessments, and machine learning forecasting methodologies.

The study combines data from MNRE, CEA, PIB, NIWE, Ministry of Power, UNFCCC, IEA, Ember, GWEC, and other authoritative sources to:

- Identify and validate official wind-energy-related targets
- Build structured, ML-ready forecasting datasets
- Forecast target achievement likelihood by 2030
- Quantify expected shortfalls and achievement gaps
- Evaluate infrastructure and policy bottlenecks
- Provide evidence-based recommendations

## Key Findings

- ✅ 50% Non-Fossil Capacity Share achieved in 2025
- ✅ Wind RPO target likely achievable
- ✅ Manufacturing localisation target likely achievable
- ⚠️ 100 GW Wind Capacity target remains at risk
- ⚠️ 500 GW Non-Fossil Capacity target remains at risk
- ⚠️ Transmission infrastructure expansion remains behind schedule
- ❌ 30 GW Offshore Wind target is unlikely to be achieved by 2030

## Study Coverage

- Historical Data: FY 2006–07 to FY 2025–26
- Forecast Horizon: FY 2026–27 to FY 2029–30
- Forecasting Methods: Polynomial Regression, ARIMA, XGBoost, Scenario Analysis
- Research Focus: Wind Energy, Grid Infrastructure, Renewable Energy Policy, Energy Transition
